8. 8tracks: 27 June 2017
https://blog.8tracks.com/2017/06/27/password-security-alert/

18 million emails and passwords stored as salted SHA-1 hashes, for users who didn't sign up with either Google or Facebook authentication

"the vector for the attack was an employee’s GitHub account, which was not secured using two-factor authentication"

8tracks does not store passwords in a plain text format, but rather uses one-way hashes to ensure they remain difficult to access. These password hashes can only be decrypted using brute force attacks, which are expensive and time-consuming, even for one password.
Passwords on 8tracks are hashed and salted, meaning that even we can’t tell you what your password is by looking at the database. Although the decryption of one particular user’s password through brute-force techniques is unlikely,
